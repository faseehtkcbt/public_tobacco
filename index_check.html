What are receivables? Once TBA ships tobacco and issues a sales invoice to the buyer, the unpaid invoice amount is assigned to the bank as security. The bank lends 85% of the invoice value (TDB) or 90% (BIC-BRED historically). When the buyer pays, the receivable is released and the loan is repaid.

How the bank checks you are within limits. The bank adds up all three security types (WHR stock + receivables + in-transit), each multiplied by 85%, plus any clean/pre-crop unsecured balance. If the total exceeds the loan outstanding, you have a surplus. If below, it is a breach and you must add collateral or repay.
